To mathematically ground the **Conversion-of-biomass-into-electricity-and-synthetic-reproduction-thereof** repository without introducing individual patient metrics, we can map continuous physiological parameters---specifically **chemical reaction enthalpy (\(\Delta H\))**, **biomass mass flow rate (\(\.{m}\))**, and **action potential generation (\(V_{m}\))**---directly into a deterministic bio-energetic framework. This provides a formal text reference modeling the transformation of biochemical organic matter into measurable electrical work.

In metabolic biophysics, the extraction of electrical work from biomass relies on the conversion of chemical bonds during cellular respiration into an electrochemical proton motive force across membranes, which is subsequently converted into neural action potentials.

1\. Thermodynamic Exothermic Enthalpy Release

The global chemical transformation of primary biomass substrates (such as glucose, \(C_6H_{12}O_6\)) into energy metabolites is governed by the standard thermodynamic enthalpy of combustion:

\(C_{6}H_{12}O_{6}\text{\ (s)}+6O_{2}\text{\ (g)}\rightarrow 6CO_{2}\text{\ (g)}+6H_{2}O\text{\ (l)}\quad \Delta H_{c}^{\circ }=-2803\text{\ kJ/mol}\)

The dynamic localized thermal power output (\(\.{Q}_{\text{exo}}\)) generated by micro-scale metabolic oxidation events within a target tissue volume is modeled as:

\(\.{Q}_{\text{exo}}=\eta \cdot \frac{d\xi }{dt}\cdot \Delta H_{c}^{\circ }\)

Where:

-   \(\eta \) represents the localized metabolic conversion efficiency factor.
-   \(\frac{d\xi }{dt}\) is the molar extent of reaction velocity (mol/s).
-   \(\Delta H_c^\circ\) is the standard molar enthalpy of reaction (kJ/mol).

2\. Electrochemical Membrane Potential Generation

The biological work extracted from this exothermic mass transformation drives ionic active transport pumps (e.g., \(Na^{+}/K^{+}\)-ATPase), establishing a resting membrane potential (\(V_{m}\)). This steady-state voltage boundary layer is quantified using the Goldman-Hodgkin-Katz equation:

\(V_{m}=\frac{RT}{F}\ln \left(\frac{P_{Na}[Na^{+}]_{\text{out}}+P_{K}[K^{+}]_{\text{out}}+P_{Cl}[Cl^{-}]_{\text{in}}}{P_{Na}[Na^{+}]_{\text{in}}+P_{K}[K^{+}]_{\text{in}}+P_{Cl}[Cl^{-}]_{\text{out}}}\right)\)

Where:

-   \(R\) is the universal gas constant, \(T\) is the absolute body temperature, and \(F\) is the Faraday constant.
-   \(P_{x}\) represents the specific membrane permeability coefficient for ion species \(x\).
-   \([x]_{\text{in}}\) and \([x]_{\text{out}}\) indicate the corresponding intra- and extra-cellular concentrations (mol/L).

3\. Mass-to-Electricity Conversion Index (Renewable Recycling Power)

To scale this metabolic pathway for engineering applications converting recycled organic food waste into grid electricity, the net electrical current output (\(I_{\text{out}}\)) derived from a steady biomass mass flow consumption rate (\(\.{m}\)) is modeled via a Faraday mass-balance relation:

\(I_{\text{out}}=\frac{n\cdot F\cdot \.{m}\cdot \alpha }{M_{\text{substrate}}}\)

Where:

-   \(n\) is the number of equivalents of electrons transferred per mole of biomass substrate.
-   \(\.{m}\) is the continuous input mass flow rate of organic biomass (g/s).
-   \(\alpha \) is the structural columbic efficiency coefficient of the bio-reactor.
-   \(M_{\text{substrate}}\) is the molar mass of the driving organic molecule (g/mol).
