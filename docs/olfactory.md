In biophysical chemistry, the initial electrostatic docking step relies on the alignment of a molecule's dipole moment vector (\(\vec{\mu }\)) relative to the electric field vector (\(\vec{E}\)) of the receptor binding pocket.

1\. Dipole Alignment Energy and Interaction Matrix

The electrostatic potential energy of interaction (\(U_{\text{dipole}}\)) governing the configuration of a volatile odorant molecule inside an olfactory receptor cavity is modeled as:

\(U_{\text{dipole}}=-\vec{\mu }\cdot \vec{E}=-||\vec{\mu }||\cdot ||\vec{E}||\cos (\phi )\)

Where:

-   \(\vert{}\vert{}\vec{\mu}\vert{}\vert{}\) is the net molecular dipole moment magnitude measured in Debyes (\(\text{D}\)).
-   \(\vert{}\vert{}\vec{\mu}\vert{}\vert{} = \sum_{j} q_j \cdot \vec{r}_j\), where \(q_{j}\) represents the partial charge of atom \(j\) located at spatial coordinate position \(\vec{r}_{j}\).
-   \(\vec{E}\) is the localized electric field vector generated by the charged amino acids inside the receptor cavity.
-   \(\phi \) is the rotational binding alignment angle.

2\. Fractional Receptor Activation Kinetics

The likelihood of a receptor cell transitioning from an inactive state to an actively transducing configuration is modeled as a functional dependent of both the local odorant partial pressure concentration (\([L]\)) and the stabilizing dipole interaction energy:

\(\theta _{i}=\frac{[L]}{K_{d,i}\cdot e^{\frac{U_{\text{dipole}}}{k_{B}T}}+[L]}\)

Where:

-   \(\theta _{i}\) represents the fractional occupancy of receptor type \(i\) (\(0 \leq \theta_i \leq 1\)).
-   \(K_{d,i}\) is the base thermodynamic dissociation constant of the channel matrix.
-   \(k_{B}T\) is the thermal energy boundary product at standard body temperature.
